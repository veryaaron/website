About the Event

What’s with the name Elsewhere?

Elsewhere was newly founded under a member-owned, non-profit association called NCA. Creating an association was essential to best serve our community and build the necessary foundations to ensure our future in the desert.

If you’re here hoping to find familiar barrios (like the Garden of Joy and Curious Creatures), and the shared ethos we all love, you’re in the right place!

Any other questions about this? Don’t hesitate to reach out on our Discord or at hello@nobodies.team.

Where will Elsewhere be?

Elsewhere 2026 will take place in the Monegros desert in northern Spain, on a site that is well suited to the event.

What about the new site? I heard about a new site?

There has been a lot of excitement around a different, larger parcel of land (also in the Monegros area) that could offer significant cost savings in future years. We are actively exploring the possibility of running an event on that land, and there is a real chance something may happen there even this year — as a separate, smaller gathering, distinct from the main July event.

For the main July 2026 event, however, we are using a site that is already prepared and ready to host us. This is a deliberate choice to keep the workload on our departments, barrios, and volunteer teams manageable. The newer parcel would require significant infrastructure work (lightning rods, land preparation, road access, permits) and a huge amount of coordination — doing all of that alongside running the main burn would stretch everyone too thin.

So: the new land is exciting and we are working on it, but the main July 2026 Elsewhere event you are buying tickets for will take place on a site that is ready for us today.

Is Elsewhere an official Burning Man Regional?

Nobodies Collective is exploring official Burning Man Regional status. The benefits include being listed on the Burning Man website, invitations to the European Leadership Summit, and access to a broader support network. Some of our members are attending the European Leadership Summit in April, and we hope to have clarity on regional status before July. Regardless of formal status, the event is grounded in the ten Burning Man principles.

Will there be a participant Code of Conduct?

Yes. You can read it here: Participant Code of Conduct. Everyone agrees to it when buying a ticket. We are also expanding participant wellness to offer year-round mediation and support, not just during the burn.

  • Discord — This is the primary hub for discussions, updates, and working groups. Join the governance channel for go/no-go updates.
  • nobodies.team website — We are publishing sanitised board meeting notes and community meeting notes here regularly.
  • Community meetings — We hold regular Thursday community meetings open to everyone.
  • This FAQ — We will keep this document updated as the situation evolves.
  • Humans app — Sign up here to register as a volunteer, access shared resources, and participate in votes.

We are working on improving and consolidating our communication channels. If you feel you are missing information, please reach out — we would rather over-communicate than leave people in the dark.

How much will tickets cost, and when will they go on sale?

Volunteer ticket sales opened on March 14th. Public ticket sales are live since March 20th. We are targeting a ticket price of approximately €275, which is broadly in line with comparable community events. Ticket revenue is what funds the event.

Will there be low-income tickets or financial assistance?

Yes. We offer reduced-price low-income tickets as part of our commitment to radical inclusion. See the Low-Income Ticket Policy for full details on eligibility and how to apply.

We are actively planning for this. We are negotiating vendor contracts to minimise non-refundable deposits, and working on keeping financial exposure low at every stage, so that if a cancellation happened, the path to refunding tickets is as clear as possible.

Getting Involved

How can I get involved?

Join our Discord to connect with the community and find out where help is needed. Then sign up on the Humans app to formally register as a collaborator and join a team. Whether you want to help with production, communications, governance, art, or anything else — those are the two places to start. We especially need people for early build work, communications, and organisational design.

What is the Humans app and do I need to sign up?

The Humans app is the official platform for the association — where you apply to become a member or collaborator, where teams are listed, and where coordination happens. If you previously signed up via Typeform, you will need to create an account on the Humans app. Going forward, it is the single place for managing involvement.

What is the difference between a member, a collaborator, and a volunteer?

There are three levels of engagement with the organisation:

  • Members (asociados) are the democratic backbone of the association. As a member, you have a statutory legal right to vote at the General Assembly, to elect and stand for the board, and to vote on decisions that the board is then legally required to carry out. These rights are enshrined in our statutes and enforced by Spanish law. Members are ultimately who the board represents, so there is an expectation that you care about and participate in the good governance of the association. Membership expires after two years and must be renewed — we want active members, not a passive registry.
  • Collaborators are people who do preparatory work for the event outside of the event itself — creating documents, designs, plans, lists, and other assets. Because they handle organisational information and create work product, collaborators sign an agreement that includes a non-exclusive but irrevocable IP assignment (so anything you create still belongs to you, but the association can always use it) and brings them under our GDPR umbrella so they can process personal data on behalf of the organisation. Collaborators do not have a statutory right to vote, but that does not mean they are excluded from decision-making — for example, we invited a much wider group (not just members) to vote on the event name and the go/no-go decision, and we intend to keep doing that wherever possible.
  • Volunteers are people who buy a ticket, come to the event, and sign up for shifts. You do not need to sign any additional agreement to volunteer — the participant Code of Conduct and terms and conditions you agree to when buying your ticket are sufficient. We do not typically ask event participants to vote on organisational matters, simply because it is difficult to track who they are outside of the event.

Both members and collaborators are managed through the Humans app. If you are doing work for the event in the lead-up, you will want to sign up as a collaborator. If you also care about governance and want a formal voice in how the association is run, consider becoming a member.

Am I personally liable if I volunteer or become a member of the association and something goes wrong financially?

No. Under Spanish association law, your personal liability is limited to your financial contribution — that is, your membership fee. If the association takes on debt, you are not personally on the hook for it. This was confirmed by our legal advisors.

Organisation & Governance

How is the association structured? Who makes decisions?

Nobodies Collective is a Spanish association (see our Estatutos) with a volunteer board elected by its members. Operationally, the event is run by Coordinators and leads in a participatory structure. The board’s long-term intention is to step back from day-to-day operations and only handle legal obligations. We are actively looking for people interested in organisational design to help build better systems for decentralised decision-making — if that is you, join the #governance channel on Discord.

How will the organisational structure work — how are Coordinators and leads selected?

We have adopted a departmental structure suited to the needs of the event. For this year, we are recruiting experienced volunteers who want to take on roles, while building toward a more democratic selection process. The board has the authority to appoint and approve Coordinators, and we are seeking input from existing leads in each area. We acknowledge the bootstrap challenge: ideally leads would elect Coordinators, but we need leads to be in place first. Different areas may approach this differently depending on their circumstances. The goal is to get the right people in place quickly for this year, then establish proper democratic processes for future years. Anyone interested in a lead or Coordinator role should make themselves known through Discord or the Humans app.

When will the board hold a new election to improve diversity and representation?

We are committed to running another board election. During the original election process, we made a commitment to improve diversity and representation on the board, and we intend to follow through. The process requires several steps: first, we need to onboard association members (membership criteria are being finalised now); then, per our statutes, we must observe a 45-day notification period before an election. We are actively working on the membership criteria and hope to begin the onboarding process soon. Critically, we need candidates — if you are interested in serving on the board and bringing diverse perspectives, please let us know. An election without candidates is not possible, so consider this a genuine call to action.

Event Operations

Will there be power/electricity on site?

Yes. Power is in good shape. Javi, our power lead, has assembled a new power team of around seven people, several with direct experience working on power infrastructure for outdoor events. They already have generator quotes in hand, a dedicated power thread on Discord under SLI, and are working through detailed role descriptions. Some experienced hands from previous years (including Blondie, Piko, and others) may also be available to support. In Javi’s words: “We are good to go. We already have a team, and we are working on it. From the power side, we are okay — green light.”

How is production equipment being secured?

Key production equipment, including containers with infrastructure and supplies, is being secured through commercial rental arrangements. This approach lets us begin production planning now while keeping things straightforward from a legal and logistical standpoint. We hope to have these arrangements finalised shortly.

I’m worried about volunteer burnout. What is being done about that?

Burnout is a top concern. We are working to simplify processes, attract new people, decentralise decision-making, and increase autonomy — to reduce the load that traditionally falls on a few overworked long-time burners. If you can contribute even a few hours during early build, it makes a real difference. If you are feeling stretched, please say so — we would rather adjust than burn people out.

What could still cancel the event?

We are committed to proceeding, but we are also realistic. The scenarios that could change the plan include: a major external disruption (pandemic, extreme weather, government restrictions), legal advice that materially changes our assessment, or dramatically low ticket sales that would make the event financially unviable. To manage financial risk, we are using a tiered budgeting approach — scaling our spending based on actual ticket revenue rather than committing to a fixed budget upfront. The board is defining specific milestones and decision points so that we can course-correct responsibly at each stage. We will make our budgeting plans public as soon as possible.

Sobre el Evento

¿Por qué el nombre Elsewhere?

Elsewhere se fundó recientemente bajo una asociación sin ánimo de lucro, propiedad de sus miembros, llamada NCA. Crear una asociación era esencial para servir mejor a nuestra comunidad y construir los cimientos necesarios para asegurar nuestro futuro en el desierto.

Si estás aquí con la esperanza de encontrar barrios conocidos (como el Garden of Joy y Curious Creatures), y el ethos compartido que todos amamos, ¡estás en el lugar correcto!

¿Tienes más preguntas sobre esto? No dudes en contactarnos en nuestro Discord o en hello@nobodies.team.

¿Dónde será Elsewhere?

Elsewhere 2026 tendrá lugar en el desierto de los Monegros en el norte de España, en un emplazamiento bien adaptado al evento.

¿Qué pasa con el nuevo emplazamiento? ¿He oído hablar de un nuevo emplazamiento?

Hay mucha ilusión en torno a una parcela diferente, más grande (también en la zona de los Monegros), que podría ofrecer un ahorro significativo en los próximos años. Estamos explorando activamente la posibilidad de organizar un evento en esa tierra, y existe una posibilidad real de que algo ocurra allí incluso este año — como una reunión aparte, más pequeña, distinta del evento principal de julio.

Sin embargo, para el evento principal de julio de 2026, vamos a usar un emplazamiento que ya está preparado y listo para acogernos. Es una decisión deliberada para mantener manejable la carga de trabajo sobre nuestros departamentos, barrios y equipos de voluntarios. La parcela nueva requeriría un trabajo de infraestructura significativo (pararrayos, preparación del terreno, acceso por carretera, permisos) y una enorme cantidad de coordinación — hacer todo eso además de organizar el evento principal agotaría a todo el mundo.

Así que: la nueva tierra es emocionante y estamos trabajando en ello, pero el evento principal de Elsewhere de julio de 2026 para el que estás comprando entradas se celebrará en un emplazamiento que ya está listo para nosotros.

Cómo Participar

¿Cómo puedo participar?

Únete a nuestro Discord para conectar con la comunidad y descubrir dónde se necesita ayuda. Luego regístrate en la app Humans para registrarte formalmente como colaborador y unirte a un equipo.

¿Qué es la app Humans y necesito registrarme?

La app Humans es la plataforma oficial de la asociación — donde solicitas ser miembro o colaborador, donde se listan los equipos y donde se coordina todo.

¿Cuál es la diferencia entre miembro, colaborador y voluntario?

  • Miembros (asociados) son la columna vertebral democrática de la asociación. Como miembro, tienes el derecho legal de votar en la Asamblea General.
  • Colaboradores son personas que realizan trabajo preparatorio para el evento. Firman un acuerdo que incluye una cesión de PI no exclusiva pero irrevocable.
  • Voluntarios son personas que compran una entrada, vienen al evento y se apuntan a turnos.

¿Soy personalmente responsable si soy voluntario o miembro?

No. Según la ley española de asociaciones, tu responsabilidad personal está limitada a tu contribución económica — es decir, tu cuota de membresía.

Organización y Gobernanza

¿Cómo está estructurada la asociación?

Nobodies Collective es una asociación española (ver nuestros Estatutos) con una junta voluntaria elegida por sus miembros. Operativamente, el evento está dirigido por Coordinadores y leads en una estructura participativa.

¿Cómo funcionará la estructura organizativa?

Hemos adoptado una estructura departamental adaptada a las necesidades del evento. Para este año, estamos reclutando voluntarios con experiencia que quieran asumir roles, mientras construimos un proceso de selección más democrático.

¿Cuándo celebrará la junta nuevas elecciones?

Nos comprometemos a celebrar otra elección de la junta. Necesitamos candidatos — si estás interesado en servir en la junta, por favor dínoslo.

Operaciones del Evento

¿Habrá electricidad en el emplazamiento?

Sí. La electricidad está en buena forma. Javi, nuestro responsable de electricidad, ha reunido un nuevo equipo de unas siete personas, varias con experiencia directa en infraestructura eléctrica para eventos al aire libre.

¿Cómo se está asegurando el equipamiento de producción?

El equipamiento clave de producción, incluidos contenedores con infraestructura y suministros, se está asegurando mediante acuerdos de alquiler comercial.

Me preocupa el agotamiento de los voluntarios. ¿Qué se está haciendo?

El agotamiento es una preocupación principal. Estamos trabajando para simplificar procesos, atraer nuevas personas, descentralizar la toma de decisiones y aumentar la autonomía.

¿Qué podría cancelar aún el evento?

Estamos comprometidos a seguir adelante, pero también somos realistas. Los escenarios que podrían cambiar el plan incluyen: una interrupción externa importante, asesoramiento legal que cambie materialmente nuestra evaluación, o ventas de entradas dramáticamente bajas.

Sull’Evento

Perché il nome Elsewhere?

Elsewhere è stata fondata di recente sotto un’associazione senza scopo di lucro, di proprietà dei membri, chiamata NCA. Creare un’associazione era essenziale per servire meglio la nostra comunità e costruire le fondamenta necessarie per garantire il nostro futuro nel deserto.

Se sei qui nella speranza di ritrovare barrios familiari (come il Garden of Joy e Curious Creatures), e l’ethos condiviso che tutti amiamo, sei nel posto giusto!

Altre domande a riguardo? Non esitare a contattarci sul nostro Discord o a hello@nobodies.team.

Dove si terrà Elsewhere?

Nel deserto di Monegros nel nord della Spagna.

E il nuovo sito? Ho sentito parlare di un nuovo sito?

C’è molto entusiasmo attorno a un appezzamento diverso, più grande (anch’esso nella zona di Monegros), che potrebbe offrire risparmi significativi nei prossimi anni. Stiamo esplorando attivamente la possibilità di organizzare un evento su quella terra, e c’è una reale possibilità che qualcosa accada là anche quest’anno — come un raduno separato, più piccolo, distinto dall’evento principale di luglio.

Tuttavia, per l’evento principale di luglio 2026, utilizzeremo un sito già preparato e pronto ad accoglierci. È una scelta deliberata per mantenere gestibile il carico di lavoro sui nostri dipartimenti, barrio e team di volontari. Il nuovo appezzamento richiederebbe un lavoro infrastrutturale significativo (parafulmini, preparazione del terreno, accesso stradale, permessi) e un’enorme quantità di coordinamento — fare tutto ciò oltre a organizzare l’evento principale sfinirebbe tutti.

Quindi: la nuova terra è entusiasmante e ci stiamo lavorando, ma l’evento principale di luglio 2026 per cui stai comprando i biglietti si terrà su un sito già pronto ad accoglierci.

Elsewhere è un Regional ufficiale di Burning Man?

Nobodies Collective sta esplorando lo status di Regional ufficiale. L’evento si basa sui dieci principi di Burning Man.

Ci sarà un Codice di Condotta?

Sì. Puoi leggerlo qui: Codice di Condotta per i Partecipanti. Tutti lo accettano al momento dell’acquisto del biglietto.

Dove trovo informazioni affidabili?

  • Discord — Hub principale per discussioni e aggiornamenti.
  • Sito nobodies.team — Note delle riunioni pubblicate regolarmente.
  • App Humans — Registrati per essere volontario.

Biglietti

Quanto costeranno i biglietti?

La vendita pubblica è attiva dal 20 marzo. Il prezzo obiettivo è di circa 275€. I ricavi dei biglietti finanziano l’evento.

Ci saranno biglietti a prezzo ridotto?

Sì. Offriamo biglietti a prezzo ridotto per persone a basso reddito come parte del nostro impegno per l’inclusione radicale. Consulta la Politica dei Biglietti a Basso Reddito per tutti i dettagli su idoneità e come fare domanda.

Cosa succede se l’evento viene cancellato?

Stiamo negoziando contratti per minimizzare i depositi non rimborsabili e mantenere l’esposizione finanziaria bassa.

Il mio codice sconto non funziona

Puoi inserirlo al secondo passaggio. Nella pagina “Details”, cerca il link “Use promo, credit or voucher code”.

Come Partecipare

Come posso partecipare?

Unisciti al nostro Discord poi registrati sull’app Humans.

Cos’è l’app Humans?

La piattaforma ufficiale dell’associazione per diventare membro o collaboratore.

Membro, collaboratore, volontario?

  • Membri — diritto di voto all’Assemblea Generale.
  • Collaboratori — lavoro preparatorio, accordo IP firmato.
  • Volontari — comprano biglietto e si iscrivono ai turni.

Sono personalmente responsabile?

No. La responsabilità è limitata alla quota associativa.

Organizzazione e Governance

Come è strutturata l’associazione?

Associazione spagnola (vedi Estatutos) con consiglio volontario eletto dai membri.

Come funziona la struttura organizzativa?

Struttura dipartimentale. Stiamo reclutando volontari esperti, costruendo verso un processo più democratico.

Quando ci saranno nuove elezioni?

Ci impegniamo a tenere un’altra elezione. Abbiamo bisogno di candidati.

Operazioni

Ci sarà elettricità?

Sì. Team di circa sette persone esperte già al lavoro.

Equipaggiamento di produzione?

Assicurato tramite accordi di noleggio commerciale.

Burnout dei volontari?

Stiamo semplificando i processi, attirando nuove persone e decentralizzando le decisioni.

Cosa potrebbe cancellare l’evento?

Interruzione esterna grave, parere legale o vendite biglietti drammaticamente basse. Approccio di bilancio a livelli.

Hai una domanda non trattata qui?

Fai la tua domanda su Discord o alla prossima riunione comunitaria (giovedì).

Über das Event

Was hat es mit dem Namen Elsewhere auf sich?

Elsewhere wurde kürzlich unter einem gemeinnützigen Verein in Mitgliederbesitz namens NCA neu gegründet. Einen Verein zu gründen war essenziell, um unserer Community besser zu dienen und die notwendigen Grundlagen zu schaffen, die unsere Zukunft in der Wüste sichern.

Wenn du hier bist in der Hoffnung, vertraute Barrios (wie den Garden of Joy und Curious Creatures) und das gemeinsame Ethos, das wir alle lieben, wiederzufinden, bist du am richtigen Ort!

Weitere Fragen dazu? Zögere nicht, uns auf unserem Discord oder unter hello@nobodies.team zu erreichen.

Wo wird Elsewhere stattfinden?

In der Wüste von Monegros in Nordspanien.

Was ist mit dem neuen Gelände? Ich habe von einem neuen Gelände gehört?

Es gibt viel Begeisterung um ein anderes, größeres Grundstück (ebenfalls in der Region Monegros), das in zukünftigen Jahren erhebliche Kosteneinsparungen bieten könnte. Wir prüfen aktiv die Möglichkeit, dort ein Event auszurichten, und es besteht eine echte Chance, dass dort sogar noch in diesem Jahr etwas stattfindet — als separate, kleinere Zusammenkunft, getrennt vom Haupt-Event im Juli.

Für das Haupt-Event im Juli 2026 nutzen wir jedoch einen Standort, der bereits vorbereitet ist und uns empfangen kann. Das ist eine bewusste Entscheidung, um die Arbeitsbelastung unserer Abteilungen, Barrios und Freiwilligen-Teams handhabbar zu halten. Das neuere Grundstück würde erhebliche Infrastrukturarbeiten erfordern (Blitzableiter, Geländevorbereitung, Straßenzugang, Genehmigungen) und einen enormen Koordinationsaufwand — das alles zusätzlich zur Durchführung des Haupt-Events würde alle überfordern.

Also: Das neue Land ist spannend und wir arbeiten daran, aber das Elsewhere-Haupt-Event im Juli 2026, für das du Tickets kaufst, wird auf einem Standort stattfinden, der bereits bereit für uns ist.

Ist Elsewhere ein offizielles Burning Man Regional?

Nobodies Collective erkundet den offiziellen Status. Das Event basiert auf den zehn Burning Man-Prinzipien.

Wird es einen Verhaltenskodex geben?

Ja. Du kannst ihn hier lesen: Verhaltenskodex für Teilnehmer. Alle akzeptieren ihn beim Ticketkauf.

Wo finde ich zuverlässige Informationen?

  • Discord — Hauptknotenpunkt für Diskussionen und Updates.
  • Website nobodies.team — Meeting-Notizen regelmäßig veröffentlicht.
  • Humans App — Registriere dich als Freiwilliger.

Tickets

Wie viel kosten die Tickets?

Der öffentliche Verkauf ist seit dem 20. März aktiv. Preis ca. 275€. Die Ticketeinnahmen finanzieren das Event.

Wird es ermäßigte Tickets geben?

Ja. Wir bieten ermäßigte Tickets für Personen mit geringem Einkommen an, als Teil unseres Engagements für radikale Inklusion. Siehe die Low-Income-Ticket-Richtlinie für vollständige Details zur Berechtigung und Antragstellung.

Was passiert bei Absage?

Wir verhandeln Verträge, um nicht erstattbare Anzahlungen zu minimieren und die finanzielle Belastung niedrig zu halten.

Mein Rabattcode funktioniert nicht

Du kannst ihn im zweiten Schritt eingeben. Auf der “Details”-Seite, suche den Link “Use promo, credit or voucher code”.

Mitmachen

Wie kann ich mitmachen?

Tritt unserem Discord bei, dann registriere dich auf der Humans App.

Was ist die Humans App?

Die offizielle Plattform des Vereins — Bewerbung als Mitglied oder Mitarbeiter.

Mitglied, Mitarbeiter, Freiwilliger?

  • Mitglieder — Stimmrecht bei der Generalversammlung.
  • Mitarbeiter — Vorbereitungsarbeit, IP-Vereinbarung.
  • Freiwillige — kaufen Ticket und melden sich für Schichten an.

Bin ich persönlich haftbar?

Nein. Deine Haftung ist auf deinen Mitgliedsbeitrag beschränkt.

Organisation und Governance

Wie ist der Verein strukturiert?

Spanischer Verein (siehe Estatutos) mit ehrenamtlichem Vorstand, gewählt von den Mitgliedern.

Wie funktioniert die Organisationsstruktur?

Abteilungsstruktur. Für dieses Jahr rekrutieren wir erfahrene Freiwillige und bauen einen demokratischeren Auswahlprozess auf.

Wann gibt es Neuwahlen?

Wir verpflichten uns zu einer weiteren Vorstandswahl. Wir brauchen Kandidaten.

Event-Betrieb

Wird es Strom geben?

Ja. Team von etwa sieben erfahrenen Personen bereits aktiv.

Produktionsausrüstung?

Gesichert durch kommerzielle Mietvereinbarungen.

Freiwilligen-Burnout?

Wir vereinfachen Prozesse, ziehen neue Leute an und dezentralisieren Entscheidungen.

Was könnte das Event absagen?

Größere externe Störung, rechtliche Beratung oder dramatisch niedrige Ticketverkäufe. Wir nutzen einen gestuften Budgetansatz.

Hast du eine Frage, die hier nicht behandelt wird?

Stelle deine Frage auf Discord oder beim nächsten Community-Meeting (donnerstags).
