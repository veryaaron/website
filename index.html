Your Temporary
Desert City

So, what is Elsewhere? Skip the corporate press release talk, this is just us building something wild, together, every summer in the Monegros desert, Spain.

It's not a festival. Let's be clear: we roll up to an empty piece of land, and over about two weeks, we build a whole temporary city from scratch. Then, we make it all vanish, leaving zero trace behind. That's the whole drill.

This Is How We Do It
(The Anti-Festival Vibe)

Forget VIP areas, stages run by companies, or people paid to manage your good time. If you see a structure, a dance floor, or a meal, that only exists because one of our friends decided to create it.

No Spectators

You don't 'attend' to consume; you commit to co-creating it. Everyone contributes.

No Money (Seriously)

We don't use cash on site. The whole place runs on gifting, mutual support, and effort. You bring what you need to survive the desert (water, food, shelter, tools, toys, gifts), and share what you can.

No Bosses

This whole thing is run by volunteers, people like you. The dream is to be totally community-owned and shaped by the people who show up.

At its core, Elsewhere is a giant social experiment. It's where art and chaos bubble up from the collective. It's creativity, connection, and collective effort, guided by our shared principles (think Gifting, Self-Reliance, Leave No Trace... the good stuff).

Curious what that actually means in practice?
Go read the principles.

Principles of Elsewhere

Our principles aren't rules imposed from above. They're commitments we hold together. They come from years of building a temporary city in the desert and are inspired by the original Burning Man principles.

Consent

The principle that holds the rest together.

Give only what's appreciated. Take only what's offered.

We're free to express, share, and co-create. But when what we do affects someone else, we ask.

Clear communication matters. Boundaries matter. Respect always matters.

Before turning up the music on your boombox, looking around your immediate area and asking the nearest people if the volume would interfere with what they are doing.

Community

A family of individuals. We look after each other.

We're all separate, self-reliant beings, but we come together because we want to be part of something bigger than ourselves. We look out for one another with tolerance, care, and a bit of joy.

Checking in on a fellow participant who has been quiet and alone for a day, even if you don't know them, and asking if they need water or just a quiet space.

Cooperation

Together we are stronger.

From raising structures to figuring things out on the fly, cooperation is at the heart of Elsewhere. If we can make something easier or better together, we do.

A group setting up a large, complicated art installation pauses their work to help a solo participant put up their tent because they see them struggling against the wind.

Gifting

We give our time, effort and gifts freely.

A cold drink. A helping hand. A weird little handmade thing. Gifts come from the heart. Nothing is expected back.

Giving away the last cold beer you brought for yourself to someone who looks overheated, and then actively refusing their offer to trade a handmade necklace for it.

Immediacy

Make now count.

Be here. Fully. This will all disappear sooner than you think, so experience it, participate in it, be in it.

Putting your phone completely away for the entire event, even to take photos, because you choose to focus on experiencing the moment directly rather than documenting it.

Inclusion

Everyone is welcome to be a Nobody.

People come as they are, and that's the point. Include others the way you'd want to be included. With respect, care, and openness.

Actively seeking out the most introverted person in your camp who hasn't spoken much, and creating space for them to share their one small, quirky contribution without pressure to be outgoing.

Leave No Trace

From dust to dust, we leave only footprints.

We take care of the land that hosts us. We clean up after ourselves and leave nothing behind. Not even the small stuff.

Not only packing out all your own trash, but also meticulously sweeping the ground where your tent stood to pick up micro-trash like glitter, broken zip ties, or stray hairs.

No Commerce

Forget about money. There's nothing to buy.

No brands. No selling. No transactions. Without money in the mix, what's left is cooperation, participation, and what actually matters.

Realizing you forgot essential sunscreen and asking around to borrow some, rather than defaulting to trying to find someone selling it for cash or barter.

Participation

Get involved. Elsewhere is what we make it.

You don't come here to watch. You come here to do. And the more you put in, the more it becomes yours.

Taking the initiative to wake up before dawn to sweep the common area of your camp or refill the communal water station without being asked.

Self Expression

The freedom to be yourself.

Be who you are. Or who you want to be. We respect that freedom and expect the same respect in return.

Wearing an outfit that makes you feel vulnerable or challenging, but consciously ensuring that your expression does not disturb the sleep or comfort of your neighbors.

Self Reliance

You are responsible for you, mentally and physically.

Bring what you need. Take care of yourself. And when you need help, ask. That's part of it too.

Bringing enough food and water for the entire trip, plus a small emergency surplus, so that if you fall ill, you do not become a burden on the collective community.

How-To-Get-In Guide

Elsewhere is built on two things: your ticket money and your sweat equity.

The cash covers the boring-but-necessary stuff like toilets, safety, site infrastructure, and art grants. This isn’t a festival run by “The Man” — it’s us. Every single ticket holder is expected to volunteer. Nobody gets paid, and nobody gets in free. Welcome to the team.
