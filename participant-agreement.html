Participant Agreement

Elsewhere 2026

This document is provided in English. An official Spanish version will be made available.

1. Why does this agreement exist?

Elsewhere is not a typical festival. It is a temporary community created by its participants in a remote and sometimes challenging environment.

This agreement exists so that everyone understands the nature of the event, the responsibilities that come with participating, and the risks that are part of the experience.

By attending Elsewhere, you acknowledge that participation in the event comes with a level of personal responsibility that may be greater than what you would normally encounter at a commercial event.

2. What kind of environment am I entering?

Elsewhere takes place in a remote outdoor environment where conditions can be unpredictable and sometimes harsh.

This may include things such as:

  • extreme heat or cold
  • dust, wind, or sudden weather changes
  • uneven terrain or limited infrastructure
  • limited access to medical services
  • long distances between services or assistance

You are responsible for preparing yourself appropriately for these conditions.

3. What does “personal responsibility” mean here?

Elsewhere operates on the principle that participants are responsible for their own wellbeing and for looking out for the people around them.

By attending the event, you agree to:

  • take reasonable steps to protect your own health and safety
  • bring the equipment and supplies you need to participate safely
  • act responsibly toward other participants
  • respect the environment and the temporary community being created
  • respect the privacy and consent of others, including when taking photos or recording

If you see someone in need of help, you are encouraged to assist or seek support from event teams.

4. What risks should I be aware of?

Participation in Elsewhere may involve risks, including but not limited to:

  • physical injury
  • environmental exposure
  • accidents involving art installations, structures, or vehicles
  • interaction with large crowds or temporary infrastructure
  • participation in physical or creative activities

By choosing to attend the event, you acknowledge that these and other risks may exist.

5. What am I agreeing to by attending?

By attending Elsewhere, you acknowledge and agree that:

  • participation in the event is voluntary
  • you are responsible for your own safety and wellbeing
  • you accept the inherent risks associated with participating in the event environment
  • you will follow the rules, policies, and guidelines established for the event

6. What happens if I don’t follow the rules?

Elsewhere depends on cooperation and respect between participants.

If you engage in behavior that creates significant risk for others or violates event rules, the organizers may deny entry to the event or ask you to leave the event site.

These decisions may be necessary to protect you and the wider community.

7. A shared experiment

Elsewhere exists because people choose to come together to build something unusual and meaningful for a short period of time.

By participating in the event, you acknowledge that this is a shared experiment in community, creativity, and responsibility.

You agree to take care of yourself, respect the people around you, and contribute to making the experience possible for everyone.

Important Note

This document is one part of Elsewhere’s participation framework. It works together with the other policies that govern participation in the event.

You should read it together with the rest of the framework, which includes:

  • Ticket Terms & Conditions
  • Refund Policy
  • Participant Agreement
  • Public Health & Safety Policy
  • Code of Conduct
  • Privacy Notice
  • Media & Image Policy

Together, these documents explain how the event works, what you can expect from the organizers, and what is expected from you as a participant. By purchasing a ticket or attending Elsewhere, you agree to this framework.
