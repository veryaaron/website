Privacy Notice — Ticketing

Elsewhere 2026 — Privacy Notice — Ticketing

This document is provided in English. An official Spanish version will be made available.

1. Why am I reading this?

When you buy a ticket for Elsewhere, we need to collect some personal information to process your registration, manage the event, and help keep the community safe.

This notice explains what information we collect, how we use it, and what rights you have over your data.

Elsewhere is organized by Nobodies Collective Association (“NCA”), which handles participant data in accordance with European data protection law (“GDPR”).

2. Who is responsible for my data?

Your personal data is handled by:

Asociación Nobodies Collective, the Organizer of Elsewhere.

If you have questions about how your data is handled, you can contact our data protection officer at: privacy@nobodies.team

3. What information do you collect about me?

When you purchase or hold a ticket, we collect some personal information such as:

  • your name
  • your email address
  • your country of residence
  • ticket purchase details
  • confirmation of payment
  • any information you voluntarily provide during the registration process

If you transfer a ticket or receive one from another participant, your information may also be processed as part of managing that transfer.

4. Why do you collect this information?

Your information is used only for purposes related to organizing and operating the event. This includes things like:

  • processing ticket purchases
  • managing ticket transfers
  • communicating important information about the event
  • managing entry to the event site
  • helping ensure participant safety and community wellbeing, investigating incidents, addressing reports of misconduct, and enforcing the event’s Code of Conduct. This may include contacting participants involved in a report and, where necessary, restricting participation in current or future events to protect community safety.
  • complying with legal and financial obligations

Your data will never be sold or used for unrelated commercial purposes.

5. Why are you allowed to process my data?

Under European data protection law, we process your data because it is necessary to:

  • fulfill the contract created when you purchase a ticket
  • operate the event and ensure participant safety
  • comply with legal and accounting obligations

6. Who can access my data?

Your information may be accessed by:

  • Nobodies Collective Association, the organization responsible for the event
  • trusted volunteers or team members who help manage participant registration
  • designated safety or consent team members responsible for handling participant wellbeing and Code of Conduct processes
  • TicketTailor, the ticketing platform used to process ticket purchases and manage registrations
  • service providers necessary to operate the event

These parties only receive the information necessary to perform their role.

7. How are my payments handled?

Ticket purchases are processed through the ticketing platform and its payment providers. The organizers do not receive or store your payment card information.

8. Where is my data processed?

Ticket purchases are processed through TicketTailor, a ticketing platform that may process data in the United Kingdom and other jurisdictions.

The United Kingdom benefits from an adequacy decision under European data protection law, meaning personal data transferred there receives a level of protection comparable to the European Union.

9. How long do you keep my data?

Your information is kept only for as long as necessary to operate the event and comply with legal obligations.

Some information related to ticket purchases and financial records may be retained for up to five years, or longer where required by law, in order to comply with accounting and legal requirements.

10. What rights do I have?

Under European data protection law, you have the right to:

  • access the personal data we hold about you
  • request correction of inaccurate information
  • request deletion of your data where legally possible
  • object to certain types of processing
  • request restriction of processing in certain circumstances

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection authority, including the Spanish Data Protection Authority (AEPD).

If you wish to exercise your rights, you can contact: privacy@nobodies.team

Important Note

This document is one part of Elsewhere’s participation framework. It works together with the other policies that govern participation in the event.

You should read it together with the rest of the framework, which includes:

  • Ticket Terms & Conditions
  • Refund Policy
  • Participant Agreement
  • Public Health & Safety Policy
  • Code of Conduct
  • Privacy Notice
  • Media & Image Policy

Together, these documents explain how the event works, what you can expect from the organizers, and what is expected from you as a participant. By purchasing a ticket or attending Elsewhere, you agree to this framework.

Questions about this notice? Contact legal@nobodies.team
