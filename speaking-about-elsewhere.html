Speaking About Elsewhere

You're excited about Elsewhere. So are we. Here are some ideas for sharing that excitement in a way that feels true to who we are.

What is Elsewhere?

Elsewhere is a participatory arts gathering in the Spanish desert, organised by Nobodies Collective, a new non-profit association. It runs July 7–12, 2026 in the Monegros desert, Spain.

It's built by volunteers. It runs on gifting, participation, and consent. There are no spectators — everyone contributes, everyone belongs.

Quick facts you can share

  • When: July 7–12, 2026
  • Where: Monegros Desert, Spain
  • Organised by: Nobodies Collective Asociación (a Spanish non-profit)
  • What to expect: Art installations, themed camps (barrios), music, workshops, desert sunrises, a temporary city built entirely by participants
  • Featuring beloved barrios: Garden of Joy, Randome, Curious Creatures, Come&Play, and many more

The vibe we're going for

When you talk about Elsewhere, you're helping shape how people feel about it before they arrive. That's a gift you're giving to the community — and it works best when it's warm, honest, and forward-looking.

Here's the spirit we'd love to see:

  • Lead with what's happening — the art, the barrios, the people, the desert. That's the real story.
  • Be inviting, not exclusive — help people picture themselves there. Elsewhere is for everyone who wants to participate.
  • Stay positive — there's so much to be excited about. Focus on what we're building, not on what came before.
  • Keep it accurate — Elsewhere is its own thing. Let it stand on its own merits.

Some examples

Try this
"Elsewhere is a participatory arts gathering in the Spanish desert — a temporary city built by the people who show up. July 7–12. Come make something beautiful."
Try this
"Garden of Joy is bringing ice cream to the desert. Curious Creatures are building their epic structure. Come&Play have ... plans... What will you bring to Elsewhere?"
Rather not
"Finally an event that does things properly." — This puts down other events and doesn't tell people anything about Elsewhere itself.
Rather not
Comparing Elsewhere to other events or defining it by what it's not. Let it speak for itself.

Self-expression and community voice

Radical self-expression is one of our core principles, and we would never ask anyone to speak from a script. You do you.

All we're saying is: when the message is about Elsewhere, the most powerful thing you can share is your genuine enthusiasm — what you're looking forward to, who you're excited to see, what you're bringing. That's what draws people in.

If someone asks you a question you can't answer, point them to our official channels — there's no pressure to have all the details.

  • Bleib positiv — es gibt so viel, worauf man sich freuen kann. Konzentriere dich auf das, was wir aufbauen, nicht auf das, was vorher war.
  • Bleib genau — Elsewhere ist etwas Eigenes. Lass es für sich selbst sprechen.
