Overview

Second community meeting of the Nobodies Collective Asociación (NCA). The meeting featured a production update, barrio survey results, a demo of the new volunteer management app, and an introduction to the community-led Governance Working Group. Significant discussion around the event name, operational readiness, and the path to a go/no-go decision.

Presentations

Daniela — Production Update

  • Daniela has begun building a vendor database, contacting suitable suppliers using publicly available information
  • Quotes received or in progress for critical services: cabins, Red Cross, generators
  • Timeline tracking based on comparable event schedules — currently on track
  • Permit process underway; Jorge is in contact with local authorities who are cooperative
  • Vendors are enthusiastic about working with the association
  • Creating a public, reusable database of vendor information for future use
  • Departments are encouraged to reach out with their specific needs

Daniel — Barrio Survey Results

  • ~100 responses received from barrio leads and camp members
  • 24 definite yes, 9 mixed (yes + don’t know), 9 don’t know, 1 no
  • Common reasons for uncertainty: lead transitions, event uncertainty creating a self-fulfilling prophecy, logistics questions, desire for a break year by some
  • Key questions from respondents: Will there be power on site? How will container fees change? Will there be low-income tickets? When do we need a firm answer?
  • Strong sense of urgency — uncertainty is causing some people to commit to other events
  • Even undecided camps expressed support for the event happening
  • Full anonymised results to be published on the website

Peter — Humans App Demo

  • New custom-built application at humans.n.burn.camp to manage volunteers and team membership
  • Google sign-in based (required for Google Drive integration)
  • Features: profile creation, consent/policy acknowledgement with audit trail, team management, resource access control
  • Automatically manages Google Drive permissions when people join or leave teams
  • Policy versioning — when policies update, all members must re-acknowledge
  • Planned: emergency contact information (board-only visibility), volunteer coordinator role
  • Addresses GDPR concerns by centralising personal data rather than spreading it across multiple Google Docs
  • Expected to go live on Sunday for broader sign-up

Kate — Governance Working Group

  • Community-led initiative to structure the go/no-go decision for 2026
  • Current members: Kate and Ella, supported by Bambi in a project management capacity
  • Seeking 1–2 additional members with expertise in process design or decentralised governance
  • Aim: non-partisan, credibly neutral process to ensure the community has relevant information
  • Developing a list of “non-negotiables” (conditions that must be met for a safe event) vs. important-but-flexible items
  • May involve a sensemaking or citizens’ assembly approach, followed by a structured vote
  • Updates will be posted in the Discord governance channel

Pablo — Administrative & Bank Update

  • Association incorporation complete; tax ID has been issued and is in transit to the lawyer
  • Bank account opening is the next step once the tax ID document arrives physically — expected within days
  • Once the bank account is open: ticket sales, vendor payments, and donations become possible
  • The association is working to secure the operational assets needed for the event
  • GDPR compliance requirements are being carefully followed

Daniel — Membership & Board Elections

  • Membership criteria still being defined — balancing inclusivity with practical governance (quorum requirements mean a very large membership body could prevent decisions)
  • Representative democracy approach under consideration: engaged community members who understand the event
  • Association membership is NOT required to participate, volunteer, lead, or attend the event
  • Board committed to running an election for additional, more diverse board members
  • Process requires 45-day notice period and onboarded members
  • Community members interested in joining the board are encouraged to make themselves known

Pablo — Event Name

  • The “Nowhere” name is not available to the association
  • A temporary event name is needed for ticket sales and communications
  • Community members invited to suggest names via a form (shared on Discord, Telegram, and other channels)
  • Top suggestions will be put to a vote among approved volunteers registered in the Humans app

Key Community Questions & Discussion

Association Independence

Several community members asked about the association’s legal standing. The board confirmed that the association is an independent legal entity, established through a democratic election process, and focused on building its own operational capability.

Why an Association?

For those joining the conversation for the first time: an association structure is the appropriate legal form for a volunteer-run community event under Spanish law. The association was founded through a democratic election in December 2025.

Why a New Name?

This generated significant discussion. Community members expressed strong attachment to the “Nowhere” name. Some participants in the meeting stated that a temporary name change could be a risk-reduction measure. Multiple creative name suggestions were offered in the chat.

Container Access

Community members emphasised that containers holding critical infrastructure (build materials, department equipment) are important for a full-scale event. Securing container access is a priority in the association’s operational planning.

Event Size and Pricing

Discussion about whether the event should be smaller this year. The board noted that fixed costs create a minimum viable scale of approximately 2,400–2,800 attendees. Target ticket price is approximately €275 (blended average including low-income and VIP tickets), similar to comparable community events. The board is actively looking for ways to reduce costs, including a 10% VAT rate (vs. 21% previously).

Lead Recruitment and Continuity

A community member asked about lead recruitment and retention, noting some Discord channels are quiet. The board acknowledged this is a priority and outlined plans for the Humans app to help organise teams and for a volunteer coordinator to help match people to roles. A departmental structure has been adopted provisionally.

Ticket Sales Timeline

Previous years typically began ticket sales in February or early March. The community expressed urgency about needing a clear commitment date, as some members need to book leave and are considering alternative events.

Community Expressions

  • Strong appreciation for the pace of progress and professionalism of the board
  • Multiple creative event name suggestions in the chat
  • Offers of practical help (camp infrastructure, build support)
  • Desire for more information and clearer communication channels
  • Suggestion to use email and WhatsApp alongside Discord, as some community members find Discord overwhelming

Key Dates Mentioned

  • Event dates (if it proceeds): July 7–12, 2026
  • Setup: Starting approximately June 15
  • Strike: Ending approximately July 22
  • Humans app launch: Expected Sunday
  • Bank account: Expected within days

Preguntas clave de la comunidad y debate

Independencia de la asociación

Varios miembros de la comunidad preguntaron sobre la situación legal de la asociación. La junta confirmó que la asociación es una entidad jurídica independiente, establecida mediante un proceso electoral democrático, y centrada en desarrollar su propia capacidad operativa.

¿Por qué una asociación?

Para quienes se unen a la conversación por primera vez: una estructura de asociación es la forma jurídica apropiada para un evento comunitario gestionado por voluntarios bajo la legislación española. La asociación fue fundada mediante una elección democrática en diciembre de 2025.

¿Por qué un nuevo nombre?

Esto generó un debate significativo. Los miembros de la comunidad expresaron un fuerte apego al nombre «Nowhere». Algunos participantes de la reunión señalaron que un cambio temporal de nombre podría ser una medida de reducción de riesgos. Se ofrecieron múltiples sugerencias creativas de nombres en el chat.

Acceso a contenedores

Los miembros de la comunidad enfatizaron que los contenedores que albergan infraestructura crítica (materiales de construcción, equipamiento de departamentos) son importantes para un evento a gran escala. Asegurar el acceso a los contenedores es una prioridad en la planificación operativa de la asociación.

Tamaño del evento y precios

Debate sobre si el evento debería ser más pequeño este año. La junta señaló que los costes fijos crean una escala mínima viable de aproximadamente 2.400–2.800 asistentes. El precio objetivo de la entrada es de aproximadamente 275 € (media ponderada incluyendo entradas de renta reducida y VIP), similar a eventos comunitarios comparables. La junta está buscando activamente formas de reducir costes, incluyendo un tipo de IVA del 10% (frente al 21% anterior).

Captación y continuidad de líderes

Un miembro de la comunidad preguntó sobre la captación y retención de líderes, observando que algunos canales de Discord están tranquilos. La junta reconoció que es una prioridad y describió planes para que la aplicación Humans ayude a organizar equipos y para que un coordinador de voluntarios ayude a emparejar personas con funciones. Se ha adoptado provisionalmente una estructura departamental.

Cronograma de venta de entradas

En años anteriores, la venta de entradas comenzaba normalmente en febrero o principios de marzo. La comunidad expresó urgencia sobre la necesidad de una fecha clara de compromiso, ya que algunos miembros necesitan reservar vacaciones y están considerando eventos alternativos.

Domande chiave della comunità e discussione

Indipendenza dell’associazione

Diversi membri della comunità hanno chiesto informazioni sullo status giuridico dell’associazione. Il consiglio ha confermato che l’associazione è un’entità giuridica indipendente, istituita attraverso un processo elettorale democratico, e focalizzata sullo sviluppo della propria capacità operativa.

Perché un’associazione?

Per chi si unisce alla conversazione per la prima volta: una struttura associativa è la forma giuridica appropriata per un evento comunitario gestito da volontari secondo il diritto spagnolo. L’associazione è stata fondata tramite un’elezione democratica nel dicembre 2025.

Perché un nuovo nome?

Questo ha generato una discussione significativa. I membri della comunità hanno espresso un forte attaccamento al nome «Nowhere». Alcuni partecipanti alla riunione hanno indicato che un cambio di nome temporaneo potrebbe essere una misura di riduzione del rischio. Nel chat sono stati offerti molteplici suggerimenti creativi di nomi.

Accesso ai container

I membri della comunità hanno sottolineato che i container che ospitano infrastrutture critiche (materiali da costruzione, attrezzature dei dipartimenti) sono importanti per un evento su vasta scala. Garantire l’accesso ai container è una priorità nella pianificazione operativa dell’associazione.

Dimensione dell’evento e prezzi

Discussione sulla possibilità di ridurre le dimensioni dell’evento quest’anno. Il consiglio ha osservato che i costi fissi creano una scala minima sostenibile di circa 2.400–2.800 partecipanti. Il prezzo obiettivo del biglietto è di circa 275 € (media ponderata inclusi biglietti a reddito ridotto e VIP), simile a eventi comunitari comparabili. Il consiglio sta cercando attivamente modi per ridurre i costi, inclusa un’aliquota IVA del 10% (rispetto al 21% precedente).

Reclutamento e continuità dei responsabili

Un membro della comunità ha chiesto informazioni sul reclutamento e la fidelizzazione dei responsabili, osservando che alcuni canali Discord sono poco attivi. Il consiglio ha riconosciuto che questa è una priorità e ha illustrato i piani per l’applicazione Humans per organizzare i team e per un coordinatore dei volontari per abbinare le persone ai ruoli. Una struttura dipartimentale è stata adottata provvisoriamente.

Tempistica di vendita dei biglietti

Negli anni precedenti, la vendita dei biglietti iniziava generalmente a febbraio o inizio marzo. La comunità ha espresso urgenza sulla necessità di avere una data di impegno chiara, poiché alcuni membri devono prenotare le ferie e stanno considerando eventi alternativi.

Wichtige Fragen der Gemeinschaft und Diskussion

Unabhängigkeit des Vereins

Mehrere Community-Mitglieder fragten nach dem rechtlichen Status des Vereins. Der Vorstand bestätigte, dass der Verein eine unabhängige juristische Person ist, die durch einen demokratischen Wahlprozess gegründet wurde und sich auf den Aufbau eigener operativer Fähigkeiten konzentriert.

Warum ein Verein?

Für diejenigen, die zum ersten Mal an der Diskussion teilnehmen: Eine Vereinsstruktur ist die angemessene Rechtsform für eine ehrenamtlich organisierte Gemeinschaftsveranstaltung nach spanischem Recht. Der Verein wurde durch eine demokratische Wahl im Dezember 2025 gegründet.

Warum ein neuer Name?

Dies führte zu einer umfangreichen Diskussion. Community-Mitglieder brachten eine starke Verbundenheit mit dem Namen „Nowhere“ zum Ausdruck. Einige Teilnehmende der Versammlung merkten an, dass ein temporärer Namenswechsel eine Risikominderungsmaßnahme sein könnte. Im Chat wurden zahlreiche kreative Namensvorschläge gemacht.

Zugang zu Containern

Community-Mitglieder betonten, dass Container mit kritischer Infrastruktur (Baumaterialien, Abteilungsausrüstung) für eine groß angelegte Veranstaltung wichtig sind. Die Sicherung des Containerzugangs ist eine Priorität in der operativen Planung des Vereins.

Veranstaltungsgröße und Preisgestaltung

Diskussion darüber, ob die Veranstaltung dieses Jahr kleiner sein sollte. Der Vorstand wies darauf hin, dass Fixkosten eine Mindestgröße von etwa 2.400–2.800 Teilnehmenden erfordern. Der Zielpreis für Tickets liegt bei etwa 275 € (Mischkalkulation einschließlich ermäßigter und VIP-Tickets), ähnlich wie bei vergleichbaren Gemeinschaftsveranstaltungen. Der Vorstand sucht aktiv nach Möglichkeiten zur Kostensenkung, darunter ein Mehrwertsteuersatz von 10 % (gegenüber zuvor 21 %).

Rekrutierung und Kontinuität der Verantwortlichen

Ein Community-Mitglied fragte nach der Rekrutierung und Bindung von Verantwortlichen und bemerkte, dass einige Discord-Kanäle ruhig sind. Der Vorstand erkannte dies als Priorität an und skizzierte Pläne für die Humans-App zur Team-Organisation und für einen Freiwilligenkoordinator zur Zuordnung von Personen zu Rollen. Eine Abteilungsstruktur wurde vorläufig eingeführt.

Zeitplan für den Ticketverkauf

In früheren Jahren begann der Ticketverkauf üblicherweise im Februar oder Anfang März. Die Gemeinschaft brachte die Dringlichkeit zum Ausdruck, ein klares Datum für eine Zusage zu erhalten, da einige Mitglieder Urlaub buchen müssen und alternative Veranstaltungen in Betracht ziehen.
