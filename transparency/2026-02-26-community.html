Community Meeting

Date: February 26, 2026 Format: Google Meet call (~1 hour 48 minutes)
Chaired by Chris Torris Olsen, with board members, leads, and community members Board members present: Daniel, Pablo, Ben, Peter, Pepe (legal advisor)

Full Records

  • Full meeting transcript
  • Meeting chat log
  • Video recording

Overview

Fourth community meeting of the Nobodies Collective Asociación (NCA). The meeting covered substantial progress on the legal transition from GNSL, a successful test of the lower-cost Ticket Taylor ticketing platform, the upcoming event name rank-choice poll, a call for community help with organizational design and decentralization, the Governance Working Group’s go/no-go information pack and community poll, legal clarifications on liability and succession risk, a power team update, the Humans App launch, participant wellness and Code of Conduct progress, and financial risk mitigation planning.

Key Topics

GNSL Transition Update (Pepe)

  • Substantial progress on the legal transition since the previous week
  • Two contracts currently under review by GNSL’s legal teams
  • Paloma and Erin coming to Barcelona the following week to work in person with the legal teams
  • First draft of written communications expected the following day, with second versions over the weekend
  • Goal: at least one contract signed by end of next week to gain access to the know-how

Ticketing System Test (Pablo)

  • Successful test of the Ticket Taylor platform as a ticketing solution
  • Drastically lower fees — potentially saving the organization \$10,000 to \$15,000
  • Test account set up with the tax ID; test purchase using Apple Pay successfully processed through Stripe
  • Card and Google Pay payments also supported

Event Name Poll (Pablo)

  • Not using the “Nowhere” name this year as the association currently lacks authorization to use it
  • 25–30 name suggestions collected from the community
  • Rank-choice poll coming in the next few days for members onboarded in the Humans app
  • Members will choose their top three options from the list

Decentralization & Organizational Structure (Daniel)

  • Emphasized the non-hierarchical, participatory nature of the event
  • The board operates by consensus — every vote has been unanimous
  • Board intends to eventually step back from operational executive input, retaining only sign-off and external-facing roles
  • Warned about the danger of power concentration and burnout caused by bootstrapping pressure
  • Called for volunteers interested in organizational design, transparency, and decentralization
  • Ben D volunteered as first point of contact for anyone wishing to contribute ideas

Go/No-Go Decision Process (Julian, Kate, Ella)

  • Governance Working Group conducted 33 interviews across 8 key areas over the past two weeks
  • Findings summarized in an information pack sent by email to 168 registered association members
  • Community poll open until 11:59 PM on Sunday night
  • Poll is non-binding — results will inform the board’s final decision
  • Tyche read a statement from Justin (Ubertown) raising concerns about the document’s neutrality, insufficient emphasis on existential risk, and the non-binding nature of the poll
  • Kate explained that a binding General Assembly would take months to organize and was not feasible given time constraints

Legal Separation & Liability (Pepe)

  • Succession risk from GNSL is very low — the NCA has different management, no shareholders, making it structurally distinct
  • Under Spanish law, member liability is limited to financial contributions (i.e. the membership fee)
  • Skipping a year of operations would not significantly reduce succession risk, as auditors can chase an audit trail for up to five years
  • Written legal opinions will be shared as part of the transaction process, covering tax, data protection, and labor risk

Power Team Update (Javi via audio)

  • New power group of approximately six people formed, many with previous experience
  • First meeting scheduled for next week after reviewing job descriptions
  • Having experienced members from previous years will facilitate progress

Humans App (Ben D)

  • Platform is now operational and serves as the official location for applications
  • Current collaborators and members being migrated to the new platform
  • Official criteria for voting membership finalized and to be posted on GitHub
  • Community will have the opportunity to adjust criteria at the next General Assembly

Participant Wellness (Anna T)

  • Focus shifting from exclusively event attendees to also covering collaborators and association members
  • Updating the participant Code of Conduct — draft to be shared in the participant wellness Discord group on Saturday for community input
  • Seeking anyone with mediation experience or interest to help create policies and provide support for disputes

Financial Risk & Refunds (Pepe)

  • Three-fold plan to mitigate financial risk: minimize non-refundable vendor deposits, expand revenue streams, and creative fundraising
  • Fundraising event confirmed for April 11
  • Aim to minimize payments until required — not much comes due before May

Action Items

  • Pablo — Send event name rank-choice poll in the next few days
  • Pablo — Share ticketing information with whoever leads ticketing
  • Ben — First point of contact for organizational design contributions
  • Ben — Organize meetings for organizational design and coordination structures
  • Anna — Share draft Code of Conduct in the participant wellness Discord group on Saturday
  • Pepe — Share full legal opinions (tax, data protection, labor risk) as the scaffolding for new operations is built
