Overview

Fifth community meeting of the Nobodies Collective Asociación (NCA). Updates on event site options, organisational infrastructure, pre-production readiness, ticket sales strategy, the board’s Go/No-Go statement, volunteer recruitment, and marketing.

Key Topics

New Site Option (Pablo)

  • Presented a potential alternative site: larger area, lower cost (~10,000 EUR for 60% of the area)
  • Would require significant infrastructure investment if used this year: lightning rods, land flattening, road construction, city planning adaptation, barrier and power system changes
  • General consensus is to continue with the current site this year but hold a Meta Leads meeting to formally evaluate
  • Future equipment purchases can be planned with the new site in mind

Legal and Organisational Infrastructure (Pepe)

  • Legal workstreams in progress: intellectual property guidelines (framework in 2–3 weeks), GDPR compliance (Pepe appointed Data Protection Officer), contractual arrangements for ticketing
  • Detailed breakdown with 3–6 month timelines to be presented at next community call

Event Name

  • The community is choosing a name for the event (26 options, poll closing 6 March)
  • Community members asked not to refer to the event using names belonging to other organisations

Membership vs. Collaborators Clarification

  • Association members must sign up on the NCA website
  • Barrio members only need basic contact information
  • FAQ posted on Discord explaining differences between members, collaborators, and volunteers

Shift to Pre-Production Mode (Daniela)

  • Symbolic shift from assessment phase to pre-production mode announced
  • All leads and interested community members called upon to increase involvement
  • Milestones and decision points to be defined the following week

Ticket Sales Strategy

  • Ticket launch targeted for the following week
  • Urgency noted: competing events going on sale around the same time
  • Relying on informal community networks for outreach
  • Tiered pricing to incentivise early purchases being discussed but not finalised
  • Organisation reserves the right to use tools like deadlines or caps if needed
  • Refund policy for early buyers being developed, tied to ticketing milestones

Go/No-Go Statement (Ben)

  • Board released a detailed statement on Discord
  • Decision to proceed was not taken lightly; full range of community opinions seriously considered
  • Moving ahead viewed as an opportunity to build strong foundations for the association
  • Board members noted their personal legal stakes, reinforcing commitment to responsible decision-making

Risk Management and Financial Planning

  • Potential scenarios discussed: pandemic, adverse legal advice, extremely low ticket sales
  • Financial risk managed through tiered budgeting: scaling expenses based on actual ticket sales
  • Milestones and decision points being defined the following week

Volunteer Recruitment

  • Focus on making it easier for new volunteers to find roles
  • ClickUp list being updated with clickable role descriptions
  • FIST volunteer platform to open before ticket sales
  • Come & Play downsizing camp activities to free capacity for NCA shifts
  • Push planned for more volunteer sign-ups from camps

Marketing and Outreach

  • No budget for paid marketing currently
  • All participants encouraged to act as ambassadors
  • Gamified referral system suggested
  • Logo competition underway; creative flyers suggested as grassroots tools

Communication Channels

  • NCA developing independent communication channels
  • Community-led groups are fair game for individual posts
  • Guidelines for communication channels being released by Pepe

Official Burning Man Regional Status (Michelle)

  • BM Regional status offers benefits including website listing, European Leadership Summit invitations, and potential support
  • NCA exploring regional status; some members attending ELS in April

Transparency and Community Participation

  • Strong community engagement noted: ~75 people on weekly calls with real decision-making access
  • Technical planning discussed: unified power grid, build optimisation, inter-grid communication, knowledge documentation
