1. City Planning and Map Launch

  • Melody P reported that city planning is in its final sprint.
  • The barrio placement map on the Humans platform opens immediately after the community call.
  • Barrio leads can add and edit their own barrio entries; all users can view the map.

  • Pablo V confirmed that a Discord voice channel would be created to support real-time placement coordination.

2. L&T — TAP Stations (Toilets)

  • Shitshow, from the L&T department, confirmed that toilets will be available at the event.
  • The stations will be known as TAP (Total Access Period). The volunteers running them will be called “tap dancers.”
  • TAP stations are intended only for those on their period, with accessibility needs, or with a stoma. Attendees are asked to communicate this to anyone coming to the event.
  • Two open questions were put to the community for feedback:
    • Should TAP stations be placed closer to the playa for night accessibility, or arranged in a spread-out layout?
    • What locking or security approach would be most resilient? Sandstorm resilience and resistance to forced removal are both concerns.
  • Feedback via Discord or the Humans app.

3. Barrio Support

  • Cayce reported that barrio registration has closed with 38 barrios registered for the year.
  • The team is now working with Peter and production on the next phase: ordering supplies for on-site use.
  • Next week’s community call (23 April) will take the form of a barrio speed-dating takeover, during which interested barrios will have approximately five minutes each to pitch their identity and vibe to attendees who have not yet joined a barrio.
  • Barrios wishing to take part can contact the barrier support team via Discord or at barrios@nobodies.team.

4. Volunteer Shortage

  • Michael M (“Nurse”) reported that the Teams system is working well and that new people have been joining and navigating it successfully.
  • However, overall volunteer numbers stand at approximately 20% of what is needed to put the event on. Pre-event build and strike weeks are the areas of greatest concern.
  • Current staffing levels by department: Build Infrastructure 14%, Cantina 26%, Power 15%, City Planning 17%, Welfare 7%, Mailfair 17%. Gates are doing well at around 40%.
  • Some shortfall may reflect attendees planning to come but not yet having signed up on Teams — teams may therefore appear emptier than they are.
  • Call to attendees: register on Teams if you are planning to attend, and encourage others to do the same.

  • Pricing will be a single standardised rate with no dynamic variable pricing; ticket changes will be free of charge if timings shift.
  • Bus tickets will go on sale the following week.
  • Aaron R noted that ticket sales are tracking well while volunteer registration is at around 20% — a clear gap requiring attention.

6. Creativity

  • Oliver K confirmed that Ziri has stepped down from the TOPS coordinator role.
  • The wider Creativity team continues to work, with exciting art proposals coming in.
  • The team’s budget is tight and additional funding would be welcome.

7. Power

  • Installation remains an uncertainty, with Power currently at 15% of needed hands. Volunteers with electrical experience are urgently needed.
  • Barrio leads placing in the green and blue zones were advised that those areas fall outside grid coverage; anyone with questions about grid access can contact power@nobodies.team.

8. Website Resources and Call for Art
