Everyone builds Elsewhere

There are no spectators at Elsewhere. No backstage passes, no VIPs, no magic lanyards. Every single participant — organisers, DJs, artists, first-timers — buys a ticket and is expected to contribute. The event exists because hundreds of people decide to cook, build, drive, dig, decorate, greet, clean, and care for each other.

Volunteering isn’t a chore you get through — it’s how you meet your people. Some of the strongest friendships at Elsewhere were forged over a shared kitchen shift at 6am, building shade structures in 35-degree heat, or cooking the last Strike dinner with whatever’s left in the pantry. You show up as a stranger; you leave as crew.

What’s it actually like?

Over three weeks before the gates open, a small crew grows from 20 people into a hundred-strong team that transforms an empty stretch of Spanish desert into a fully functioning town — complete with neighbourhoods, a power grid, 24-hour emergency services, a recycling centre, an info point, and as many dance floors as you could want.

The work is real: digging cable trenches in the sun, unloading shipping containers, GPS-mapping the city layout, feeding 80 people three meals a day. But so is the reward. Pre-event Saturday nights turn into parties under the stars. Siestas happen in the shade of structures you helped build. Cantina (the crew kitchen) becomes the social heart of the whole operation. And by the time 3,000 people walk through the gate, you already know this place is yours — because you built it.

You don’t need experience. Most of us have found ourselves installing a sound system, operating heavy machinery, cooking for a hundred people, or interpreting for local authorities — and loving every minute of a job we didn’t even know existed before Elsewhere. All you need is a can-do attitude and a willingness to work as a team.

Find your thing

There’s a role for every kind of person. Browse what’s available on Humans, or show up as a general volunteer and we’ll help match you to a team on site.

Physical work in the sun

Infrastructure, city planning, power trenches, leave-no-trace installation. All abilities welcome — you don’t need to be a builder.

Taking care of people

Cantina (cooking for the crew), fluffing (hydration runs and snack stations), or volunteer welfare. The kitchen is the heart of the event.

Meeting & welcoming people

Gate and greeters, barrio support, artist support, No Info & Ice. Be the first friendly face after a long drive.

Tech & geekery

Wi-Fi network, radio comms, power grid, sound & lighting, GPS city mapping. Use your brain and your soldering iron.

Logistics & coordination

Production, transport, heavy machinery, purchasing runs, admin. Keep the operation moving behind the scenes.

Strike (post-event)

Take everything down and return the desert to its natural state. Many volunteers say Strike is their favourite part — small crew, desert moonlight, incredible meals from leftovers.

What we provide

  • Meals — breakfast, lunch, and dinner at Cantina while you’re working with us
  • Hydration — water, juice, rehydration drinks, and snacks throughout the day
  • Cold beers at siesta and after work
  • Werkhaus — a communal shade structure to rest, socialise, and camp next to
  • Train station pickups twice daily (morning and afternoon)
  • Tools and safety equipment for the work you’re doing
  • Days off — if you’re on site for multiple weeks, we’ll sort time off for laundry, shopping, or a trip to the pool
  • Early entry to the event
  • Cruz Roja (Red Cross) on site during set-up and strike
